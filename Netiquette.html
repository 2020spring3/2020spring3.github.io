Social Media Netiquette

By: Student #3

A Short History

At it's core Social Media is essientally a form of long distance communication that can be seen publically. Since it can be defined so broadly some may say the first form of it was in 1792, when the telegraph was invented. The first public website that allowed you to do things such as make a profile and add friends was called "Six Degrees" and was created in 1997. In 1999 "blogging" started to become a trend and led many new forms of social media that are still used today.

Importance of Social Media Netiquette

Social Media is one of the most common ways of communication today and has a very strong influence on society. One of the biggest reasons that it has kept this role is since anyone can use it to make an impact. Everyone can see what is posted or said and once seen it cannot be undone. Thus, it is important to be respectful to others and leave a good record on social media.

10 Rules of Social Media Netiquette

  1. Just as if you were talking to someone in person, be polite and respect others opinions.
  2. Clarify your emotions towards topics since others cannot see your facial expressions.
  3. Before you post or send something stop and think if it is the right thing you want to say and how you want to convey it.
  4. Never completely trust someone on the internet unless if you know them in person.
  5. Be constructive instead of destuctive, especially if it is not your work.
  6. Do not claim something is yours when it is not.
  7. Be open to new ideas and be adventurous.
  8. Respect the fact if someone does not want to communicate with you and refrain from spamming them.
  9. Always check your spelling and prevent yourself from typing in full capital letters.
  10. There are many people with strong opinions on the internet. Therefore, don't share your opinions on controversial topics to avoid arguments and toxic behavior.

As long as you follow these 10 rules of social media netiquette, you should have a much safer and happy experince with social media. From here social media is yours to explore and to find a way to help it impact your life and others too!
